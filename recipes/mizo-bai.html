Mizo Bai

DESCRIPTION:

Mizo bai is a popular dish from the Mizoram region of India. It is a hearty stew made with a variety of vegetables, including leafy greens, beans, and potatoes. The stew is typically flavored with ginger, garlic, and chili peppers. Mizo bai is often served with rice.

A comforting and healthy medley of boiled vegetables, Mizo Bai often features leafy greens, beans, and root vegetables like potatoes. The flavors are subtle, highlighting the natural taste of the ingredients, with a hint of chili for warmth. It's a light and nourishing dish, perfect for a simple meal, and is traditionally served with rice.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 bunch leafy greens (spinach, mustard greens, or a mix)
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and halved
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium carrot, chopped (optional)
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp fermented soybean paste (Akhuni) or miso paste/a pinch of baking soda
  • 1 small bunch of fresh coriander leaves, chopped (or a few basil leaves as a substitute for Lengmaser)
  • 2 cups water (or more as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving
