Mizo Bai

DESCRIPTION:

Mizo bai is a popular dish from the Mizoram region of India. It is a hearty stew made with a variety of vegetables, including leafy greens, beans, and potatoes. The stew is typically flavored with ginger, garlic, and chili peppers. Mizo bai is often served with rice.

A comforting and healthy medley of boiled vegetables, Mizo Bai often features leafy greens, beans, and root vegetables like potatoes. The flavors are subtle, highlighting the natural taste of the ingredients, with a hint of chili for warmth. It's a light and nourishing dish, perfect for a simple meal, and is traditionally served with rice.

INGREDIENTS:

  • 1 bunch leafy greens (spinach, mustard greens, or a mix)
  • 1 cup green beans, trimmed and halved
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 1 medium carrot, chopped (optional)
  • 4-5 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp fermented soybean paste (Akhuni) or miso paste/a pinch of baking soda
  • 1 small bunch of fresh coriander leaves, chopped (or a few basil leaves as a substitute for Lengmaser)
  • 2 cups water (or more as needed)
  • Salt to taste
  • Cooked rice, for serving

    • STEPS:

    1. Prepare the vegetables:Wash all the vegetables thoroughly. Chop the leafy greens roughly.
    2. Combine ingredients:In a medium-sized pot, combine the potatoes, carrots (if using), green beans, garlic, ginger, green chilies, and fermented soybean paste (or substitute). Add the water and bring to a boil.
    3. Cook the vegetables:Reduce the heat to medium-low, cover the pot, and let the vegetables simmer until they are almost tender, about 15-20 minutes.
    4. Add leafy greens:Add the chopped leafy greens to the pot and cook until they wilt, about 5 minutes.
    5. Season and finish:Season with salt to taste. Add the chopped coriander leaves (or basil). Stir well and cook for another minute.
    6. Serve:Serve the Mizo Bai hot with cooked rice.

    TYPES AND VARIATIONS:

    • You can add other vegetables like cauliflower florets, eggplant, or pumpkin.
    • Adjust the amount of chili peppers to your preference.
    • If you don't have fermented soybean paste, miso paste or a pinch of baking soda will add a similar umami flavor.
    • If you like a richer flavor, you can add a small piece of pork or a spoonful of Mizo cheese (fermented pork product) while the vegetables are cooking.
    • Don't overcook the vegetables; they should retain some texture.
    • This recipe is a basic version, and you can adapt it to your liking.

    Enjoy your homemade Mizo Bai!
